From Ambiguous Text to Accurate Diagnosis: A Deep Dive into Building an AI-Powered Repair Assistant

Abstract: The proliferation of consumer electronics and IoT devices has created a significant challenge in the repair and maintenance sector. Users often describe device failures in ambiguous, non-technical language, making automated diagnosis difficult. This article chronicles a project aimed at solving this problem by leveraging artificial intelligence...

1. Introduction: The Problem of Ambiguous Fault Reports

Anyone who has experienced a device malfunction is familiar with the challenge of describing the problem. A user might report that their laptop is "making a weird noise" or their phone screen is "flickering". For a human technician, these descriptions are a starting point for a series of diagnostic questions. For an automated system, they are a significant hurdle.

User-generated fault reports are inherently ambiguous, unstructured, incomplete, and lack technical precision. This "noisy" text is difficult for traditional software to parse, making it a challenging classification problem...

2. The Research Breakthrough: Validating the Approach with LLMs

The initial phase of the project involved exploring whether modern AI, specifically Large Language Models (LLMs), could be the key to understanding this ambiguous text. This exploration led to a pivotal discovery: a research paper titled, "Evaluating LLMs and Prompting Strategies for Automated Hardware Diagnosis from Textual User-Reports".

2.1 Key Findings from the Study

The researchers conducted an extensive benchmark of 27 open-source LLMs, testing their ability to diagnose faults based on a specialized dataset called `FACTO`. The `FACTO` dataset is a collection of 853 user-generated reports, each cleanly labeled with the specific faulty hardware component (e.g., "video card," "memory," "network").

The study's most significant finding was the identification of a model that offered an exceptional balance between performance and efficiency.

`gemma-2-2b-it` achieved an impressive F1-score of 0.7269 with only 2 billion parameters. This demonstrated that a lightweight, efficient model could achieve high accuracy on this specialized task, making it a feasible and cost-effective choice for a real-world application.

This discovery was the cornerstone of our implementation plan. It proved that we didn't need a massive, resource-intensive model to build a viable product.

3. The Implementation Plan: Building the V1.0 Diagnostic Engine

Armed with this validation, we formulated a detailed technical roadmap for our Minimum Viable Product (MVP). The plan focuses on building a robust diagnostic model by combining the strengths of multiple datasets and leveraging state-of-the-art training techniques.

3.1 The Data Strategy: A Tale of Two Datasets

While the `FACTO` dataset is perfect for its high-quality labels, its small size is a limitation. To build a model that can understand the vast diversity of user language, we needed more data. This led us to the Open Repair Alliance (ORA) dataset.

Our solution is to use a hybrid approach:

  • Ground Truth: Use the `FACTO` dataset as our accurately labeled "ground truth."
  • Linguistic Enrichment: Use the text from the ORA dataset's `problem` column as a source for data augmentation.

3.2 Data Augmentation: Making a Small Dataset Mighty

Data augmentation is a set of techniques used to increase the size and diversity of a training dataset... Key techniques include:

  • Contextual Word Replacement: Replacing words in `FACTO` sentences with contextually similar words found in the ORA dataset...
  • Back-Translation: Translating a sentence to another language and then back to English...

3.3 Model Training: Fine-Tuning `gemma-2-2b-it`

With our augmented dataset, the final step is to fine-tune the pre-trained `gemma-2-2b-it` model... we will use Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) techniques like QLoRA (Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation).

3.4 Model Inference: From Text to Diagnosis

Once fine-tuned, the model will be deployed as a secure API. The inference process will be straightforward...

4. The Proposed Solution: V1.0 "The Diagnostic Engine"

The culmination of this work is a focused MVP designed to prove the core technology and begin a virtuous cycle of data collection.

  • The Product: A simple web application where a user selects their device...
  • The Experience: In seconds, the user receives a clear, concise diagnostic report.
  • The Business Model: A simple pay-per-diagnosis model will be used...

5. Conclusion and Future Work

The journey from a broad concept to a concrete, technically validated plan highlights the power of leveraging academic findings to de-risk and accelerate a startup project. The discovery that a lightweight model like `gemma-2-2b-it` can perform this complex classification task with high accuracy was a critical turning point.
